# CONSTRUCTED GUILT
## Executive Summary

### Why This Research Matters

**The people who sound most guilty are most likely to be telling the truth.**

This thesis demonstrates, through converging evidence from psychology, neuroscience, linguistics, and criminology, that the criminal justice system does not discover guilt — it constructs it. The system's methods for assessing credibility are not merely imperfect; they are *inverted*. Authentic behaviour triggers suspicion. Performed confidence is mistaken for honesty. The result: innocence provides no reliable protection against conviction.

## The Core Finding: The Signal Inversion Effect

| What You Do | What It Means | What They Think |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Look away while thinking | Cognitive effort; accessing memory | Shifty, hiding something |
| Say "I think" or "maybe" | Genuine uncertainty; honest retrieval | Evasive; lying |
| Tell a fragmented story | How real memory works, especially trauma | Fabricating; inconsistent |
| Say "I don't know" | Accurate self-knowledge | Uncooperative; guilty |
| Speak confidently, fluently | Rehearsed narrative; possible deception | Honest; credible |

**This inversion is not a bug. It is the system's operating condition.**

## The Numbers That Should Stop Any Trial

### Deception Detection: Trained Investigators Are Guessing

- **54.1% accuracy** — barely above a coin flip (50%)
- **37,500 judgments** analysed across 247 studies
- **Training doesn't help** — it just increases confidence in wrong assessments
- Police, judges, psychiatrists: all perform at essentially chance level

*Source: Bond & DePaulo (2006), meta-analysis of 247 studies*

### Memory Is Rewritten by Questions

- **22% of people** will have their memory altered by a single misleading question
- **d = 0.72** — a large effect size (larger than most psychology findings)
- Changing one word ("smashed" vs "contacted") produces false memories of broken glass in 32% of subjects
- Cross-examination doesn't test memory — it edits it

*Source: Loftus (2005), 30-year research programme*

### False Confessions Are Common

- **12–30%** of exonerations involve false confessions
- **27 of the first 250 DNA exonerees** had provided detailed, signed confessions to crimes they did not commit
- **42% of false confessors** are juveniles
- These are floors, not ceilings — they capture only cases where exoneration succeeded.

*Source: National Registry of Exonerations; Garrett (2011)*

### Pre-Interrogation Detention Doubles Suggestibility

- Arrest + detention + sleep disruption + isolation = **+80–120% increase in suggestibility**
- The person entering the interview room is *neurobiologically different* from the person arrested
- Yet the law treats their statements as "voluntary"

*Source: Gudjonsson (2003); Starcke & Brand (2012)*

## Why Heuristics Cannot Work — Regardless

The thesis demonstrates that credibility heuristics are unreliable **regardless of training, experience, or refinement**, for three independent reasons:

### 1. The Baseline Is Near-Random

The empirical accuracy rate of 54.1% means the system operates at essentially chance level. You cannot build reliable outcomes on a foundation of noise. Even if individual cues had some validity, the compound error rate across multiple assessments — investigator → prosecutor → jury — ensures unreliable final outcomes.

### 2. The Heuristics Are Inverted

The specific cues people use to assess credibility (gaze aversion, hedging, fragmented narrative) are *negatively correlated* with deception. The folk psychology of lying is not merely inaccurate — it points in the wrong direction. Training in these cues makes assessors *worse*, not better.

### 3. The Feedback Loop Destroys Any Residual Signal

Even if some heuristics had partial validity, the **Behavioral Adaptation Feedback Loop** would progressively destroy that validity:

1. **Authentic behaviour is disbelieved** → Person learns their natural presentation attracts suspicion
2. **Person adopts "honest" behaviours** → Maintains eye contact, speaks fluently, projects confidence
3. **These are the behaviours of actual liars** → Rehearsed, performed, strategically deployed
4. **Detection becomes impossible** → Honest people have been trained to present like deceivers

The trap is inescapable: authentic presentation triggers suspicion; performed presentation triggers suspicion; awareness of the problem triggers suspicion.

**This means the system cannot self-correct.** No amount of training or refinement can fix a methodology built on inverted heuristics that honest people actively learn to circumvent.

## The Neurodivergent Double Bind

### Autism, FND, ADHD, PTSD: Built to Be Disbelieved

Neurodivergent individuals present authentically in ways the system is specifically calibrated to misread as deception:

| Neurodivergent Presentation | System Interpretation |
|-----------------------------|----------------------|
| Reduced eye contact (autistic sensory management) | Shifty, untrustworthy |
| Flat affect (autistic/PTSD/FND) | Cold, no remorse, faking |
| Direct communication without hedging | Aggressive, uncooperative |
| Fragmented trauma narrative (PTSD/CPTSD) | Inconsistent, fabricating |
| Variable symptoms (FND) | Faking illness |
| Says "I don't know" when genuinely uncertain | Evasive, hiding something |
| Corrects interviewer's errors | Argumentative |

### The Compound Vulnerability

The neurodivergent person in the criminal justice system faces:

- **Pre-interrogation conditions** that are particularly damaging (sensory overwhelm, routine disruption, stress-exacerbated symptoms)
- **Reid Technique interpretation** that codes their baseline behaviours as deceptive
- **Courtroom presentation** that reduces credibility with juries applying neurotypical schemas
- **Media framing** that narrativises atypical affect as "cold," "unfeeling," or "showing no remorse"

**The system is not failing neurodivergent people. It is processing them exactly as designed.**

## What This Means

### For Individuals

If you:
- Have autism, ADHD, FND, PTSD, or CPTSD
- Communicate directly without social performance
- Look away when thinking
- Say "I don't know" when you don't know
- Have symptoms that vary in intensity
- Don't perform distress in expected ways

Then the system will read your authentic presentation as evidence of deception, regardless of your innocence.

### For the System

The criminal justice system is not a truth-finding mechanism that sometimes fails. It is a **guilt-production mechanism** that maintains the rhetorical apparatus of truth-finding. Its operational purpose is conviction. Its legitimating narrative is justice. These are not the same thing.

The presumption of innocence is not a description of how the system operates. It is a **performative speech act** — a legal utterance that constitutes a protection in language that the institutional architecture consistently overrides in practice.
