Nikola Tesla envisioned a system of "free energy"—meaning sustainable, wireless, and widely accessible electricity—by harnessing the Earth's natural electromagnetic currents and atmospheric electricity. His ultimate goal was to transmit power globally via the ionosphere using his wireless technology, famously tested at Wardenclyffe Tower. 
Key Aspects of Tesla’s Energy Vision:

    Wireless Transmission: Tesla aimed to transmit electrical power through the ground and atmosphere, removing the need for cables.
    "World System": He proposed a global, wireless power grid to provide energy to humanity, aiming to eliminate the need for burning fossil fuels.
    Radiant Energy: He held patents for utilizing "radiant energy," which could capture electrical energy from the environment.
    Atmospheric Power: Tesla believed in tapping into "fluid electrical charges" within the Earth and atmosphere, a concept sometimes linked to modern, unproven concepts like zero-point energy. 

Why the Dream Was Never Realized:

    Funding and Economic Disruption: His primary investor, J.P. Morgan, withdrew funding when it became clear this system would make electricity free and hard to monetize.
    Scientific Feasibility: While brilliant at electrical engineering, Tesla's theories on global energy distribution were not technically viable with the technology available, leading to the failure of the Wardenclyffe project.
    Competition: His work faced opposition from rivals and mainstream scientific skepticism, say Quora users and Reddit users. 

Although his "free energy" dream did not materialize, Tesla’s pioneering work on wireless electricity demonstrated principles later used in modern technology.
