 OK. Here's where each of the four problems actually stands:

  ---
  Problem 1: Interference (Tesla jammed all radio frequencies)

  This one's actually solved. Tesla used spark-gap transmission — basically controlled lightning — which sprays energy across every frequency. Modern systems
  don't do this. They use narrow-band resonant coupling at specific frequencies (like 13.56 MHz). Frequency Selective Surfaces and metamaterials can further
  contain the signal to a tight band. This is how wireless phone chargers work next to your WiFi without killing it. The interference problem is a 1899
  problem, not a 2026 problem.

  Verdict: Solved. Modern resonant systems don't create broadband interference.

  ---
  Problem 2: Efficiency drops off with distance

  This is the hard one. Current state of the art:
  - 50cm: 88% efficiency (250W)
  - 2 metres: 46% efficiency (50W)
  - 8.6km: 20% efficiency (800W, DARPA microwave beam)

  The dropoff is steep. BUT — there's a promising approach: relay resonator chains. You put intermediate coils between transmitter and receiver, each one
  resonating at the same frequency. Like a bucket brigade for electricity. A 2024 study showed up to 20 relay coils transferring power over 4 metres with high
   efficiency. The principle scales — you'd just need relay stations, like cell towers but for power.

  That's not "wireless" in the Tesla sense of beaming power through the Earth. It's more like invisible power lines made of resonant coils. But it eliminates
  the physical wires.

  Verdict: Unsolved at long range. Relay chains are the most promising path. Nobody's testing them at kilometre scale yet.

  ---
  Problem 3: Is Earth resonance global or local?

  This is where it gets really interesting. The Schumann resonance (7.83 Hz) IS global — confirmed, measured, real. Lightning strikes excite it naturally all
  the time. The Earth-ionosphere cavity is a real resonant structure.

  BUT — the Q factor (how well it holds energy) is only about 5-10. For power transmission you'd need a Q of about 1,000,000. That's a gap of 100,000×. The
  cavity leaks energy too fast for power transfer.

  HOWEVER — HAARP has artificially excited Schumann resonances. They heated a patch of ionosphere with 3.6 MW of radio waves and successfully created
  large-amplitude waves at 7.8-8.0 Hz. In 8 out of 46 attempts. By locally changing the ionosphere's conductivity.

  So: the Earth cavity IS global, you CAN artificially excite it, but its Q factor is far too low to accumulate enough energy for power delivery. Tesla's
  intuition was right about the resonance. His assumption that it could carry power was wrong — the cavity is too leaky.

  Verdict: Earth resonance is real and global. But the cavity leaks energy ~100,000× too fast for power transmission. Unless someone figures out how to raise
  the Q factor.

  ---
  Problem 4: Containing the discharge (no 135-foot lightning)

  Modern Tesla coils use toroidal top-loads that direct arcs outward and away from equipment. Faraday cages contain the electromagnetic field. Arc filters
  (inductors) dampen current peaks and reduce uncontrolled discharge. Solid-state Tesla coils (no spark gap) produce controlled, predictable output without
  the violent discharge.

  For a real power system you wouldn't use Tesla coil architecture at all. Modern resonant coupling systems produce no visible arcing — your phone charger
  doesn't shoot lightning. The dangerous discharge was a consequence of spark-gap technology, not of wireless power transfer itself.

  Verdict: Solved. Modern resonant systems don't produce uncontrolled discharge. The lightning was a feature of 1899 technology, not of the underlying
  principle.

  ---
  So where does that leave it?

  Two problems solved (interference, discharge safety). One partially solved (distance — relay chains work but untested at scale). One genuinely hard (Earth
  cavity Q factor too low by 5 orders of magnitude).

  The most realistic path isn't Tesla's grand vision of one transmitter powering the planet. It's a mesh network of resonant relay stations — local power
  generation (solar/wind) transmitted wirelessly over short-to-medium distances via resonant coupling chains. No wires, no central grid, no metering monopoly.
   Each node generates and relays. Basically your BLE mesh networking concept, but for electricity instead of data.
