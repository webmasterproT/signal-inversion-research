PILLAR I: DECEPTION DETECTION — THE CHANCE PROBLEM
Source: Bond & DePaulo (2006), k = 206 studies, N = 24,483
================================================================================

  Overall accuracy: 54.0% (vs 50% chance)
  Truth detection:  61.0% (truth bias — people tend to believe)
  Lie detection:    47.0% (BELOW chance)

  Z-test vs chance: z = 12.52, p = 0.000000
  Cohen's h = 0.080 (trivially small; h < 0.20 = negligible)
  Advantage over chance: 4.0 percentage points

  Professional groups (Bond & DePaulo 2006):
    Police officers:    ~55%  (no better than public)
    Customs officials:  ~55%
    Judges:             ~54%
    CIA/Secret Service: ~64%  (Ekman & O'Sullivan 1991, N=34, not replicated)

  CONCLUSION: Statistically significant but practically negligible.
  The system operates functionally at chance for lie detection (47%).
  Training does not help. Confidence does not correlate with accuracy.
