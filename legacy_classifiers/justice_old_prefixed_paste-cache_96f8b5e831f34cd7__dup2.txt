                                                                                          
  The contracts are fine. HER has bootstrapVerify(), vouch() (auto-verifies at 3), trust
  scoring with ripple responsibility. OMXUS has claimBasicShare(), revenue sharing. VoteAussie 
  has EIP-712 batch voting. All the on-chain pieces exist.
                                                                                               
  The wallet is the bottleneck. Every problem traces back to app.js/identity.js:             

  1. Your token problem — the wallet only offers "type your passphrase" or "paste a hex private
   key." If you have a HER token on an address but forgot the exact words, or if the address
  came from MetaMask, there's no path. The fix is a 3-path login: passphrase,
  MetaMask/WalletConnect, or read-only (paste address to at least see your status).
  2. The vouch ceremony is fake — confirmVouch() is literally a toast message. The contract's
  vouch() function works perfectly, the wallet just never calls it. BLE discovers people but
  clicking "vouch" does nothing on-chain.
  3. Key storage is dangerous — private key stored as raw hex in localStorage. The fix: don't
  persist the key at all. Store only the address for read-only state. Re-derive from words when
   the user wants to sign something.
  4. Login doesn't verify — wrong words = different identity, silently. Need to show the
  derived address and check it against on-chain state before proceeding.
