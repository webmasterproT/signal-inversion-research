STUDY 4: HUMAN vs ALGORITHMIC DECEPTION DETECTION — CONVERGENCE
================================================================================

Human Detection Accuracy (Bond & DePaulo, 2006; k=206 studies, N=24,483)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Human lie detection              47.0%
  Human overall                    54.0%
  Police officers                  55.0%
  Customs officials                55.0%
  Judges                           54.0%

Algorithmic Detection Accuracy (linguistic features)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Algorithm disfluency only        60.3%  (Current Study 1)
  Algorithm multi-variable         63.5%  (Current Study 1)
  Algorithm 3-predictor            78.5%  (Rizzelli et al 2021)

======================================================================
GAP ANALYSIS
======================================================================
  Chance level:               50.0%
  Human overall:              54.0%
  Human advantage over chance: 4.0 percentage points

  Algorithm disfluency only       gap = +6.3pp over human  (60.3% vs 54.0%)
  Algorithm multi-variable        gap = +9.5pp over human  (63.5% vs 54.0%)
  Algorithm 3-predictor           gap = +24.5pp over human  (78.5% vs 54.0%)

  CRITICAL: Human lie detection = 47.0% — BELOW chance (50.0%)
  Humans are worse than coin-flipping at detecting lies specifically.

  Cohen's h (human overall vs chance) = 0.080
  h < 0.20 = negligible effect size
  The 4-point advantage is statistically significant (N=24,483)
  but practically meaningless — a system built on this is random.

======================================================================
WHY THE GAP EXISTS
======================================================================

  Humans rely on cues that are:
    - Unrelated to actual deception (gaze aversion, fidgeting)
    - Actively inverted (disfluency is HIGHER in truth-tellers)
    - 78.6% empirically wrong (Study 3 inversion matrix)

  Algorithms use cues that are:
    - Linguistically measurable (pronoun rates, conjunction patterns)
    - Imperceptible to human observers in real-time
    - Empirically validated against ground truth

  The features humans CAN perceive are inverted.
  The features that WORK are imperceptible.
  This is the Signal Inversion Effect operating at the system level.

======================================================================
TRAINING EFFECT: NONE
======================================================================
  Police officers:      55%  (trained)
  Customs officials:    55%  (trained)
  Judges:               54%  (decades of experience)
  General public:       54%  (untrained)

  Training does not improve deception detection (Bond & DePaulo 2006).
  Confidence does not correlate with accuracy (DePaulo et al. 1997).
  Experience does not help because the training reinforces inverted cues.
