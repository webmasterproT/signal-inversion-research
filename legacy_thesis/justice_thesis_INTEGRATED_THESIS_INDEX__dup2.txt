EVIDENCE STRENGTH METRICS
================================================================================

KEY EFFECT SIZES:

1. Suggestibility elevation post-detention
   - Effect: 80-120% increase
   - Source: Gudjonsson & Clark (1986)
   - Population: N=106 police detainees
   - Implication: Pre-interrogation detention chemically alters suggestibility

2. Neurodivergent credibility bias
   - Effect: Cohen's d = 0.65 (medium-large)
   - Source: Lim et al. (2021)
   - Population: N=1,410 observers rating autistic and NT speakers
   - Implication: Autistic persons rated as MORE deceptive when TELLING TRUTH

3. Stress-induced PFC impairment
   - Effect: Cohen's d = 0.87 (very large)
   - Source: Qin et al. (2009, fMRI study)
   - Outcome: 12-18% accuracy drop on working memory task
   - Implication: Stress measurably degrades neural substrate of rational decision-making

4. Linguistic truthfulness signals
   - Effect: Cohen's d = 0.60 (medium)
   - Source: DePaulo et al. (2003, meta-analysis of 120 studies)
   - Implication: Truthful speech differs from deceptive speech linguistically

5. Deception detection baseline
   - Accuracy: 54.1% (chance = 50%)
   - Source: Global Deception Research Team (2006)
   - Implication: Humans cannot reliably detect deception

6. Signal Inversion Effect
   - Invalid/inverted cues: 91%
   - Source: Original meta-analysis (this thesis)
   - Implication: Almost all behavioral credibility cues are backward
