# What This Proves

## What we tested

We compared how well humans detect lies versus how well computers do it using linguistic features (word patterns that humans can't consciously perceive). We used data from the largest meta-analysis on human lie detection ever conducted — 206 studies, 24,483 participants (Bond & DePaulo, 2006).

## What we found

Humans detect lies at **54%** accuracy. A coin flip is 50%. That 4-point gap is statistically significant because the sample is enormous, but practically it's nothing — you'd be almost equally well served flipping a coin.

It gets worse. Humans detecting **lies specifically** (not just truth) score **47%** — actually below chance. People are worse than random at spotting when someone is lying to them.

Computers using linguistic features (word rates, pronoun patterns — stuff invisible to the naked ear) reach **63.5-78.5%** accuracy. The gap exists because:

- The cues humans **can** see are inverted (Study 3 showed 74% wrong)
- The cues that **work** are imperceptible to humans in real-time
- Training doesn't help — police, judges, customs officers all score about the same as the general public (54-55%)

## Why it matters

The justice system trusts human judgment about who is lying. Judges have "decades of experience." Police are "trained in deception detection." None of it works. Training actually makes things worse in one sense: it makes people more confident without making them more accurate. Confidence and accuracy are uncorrelated (DePaulo et al., 1997).

## The numbers (simplified)

- **54%** — human lie detection accuracy (coin flip = 50%)
- **47%** — human accuracy at detecting lies specifically (below chance)
- **63.5%** — computer accuracy using linguistic features
- **24,483** participants across **206 studies**
- **55%** — police officers (no better than the public)
- **54%** — judges (decades of experience, no improvement)

## So what?

The people deciding whether you're telling the truth are performing at chance level. They just don't know it — and neither does the system that gave them the authority.
