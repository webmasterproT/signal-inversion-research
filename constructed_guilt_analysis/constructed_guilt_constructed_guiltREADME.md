# Constructed Guilt Thesis (Canonical Manuscript Package)

Organised **thesis source tree**: manuscript chapters and supplements (`manuscript/`), references, data, compiled outputs, and `src` for build or export workflows. It is the structured home for the argument that criminal justice **produces** guilt through language, procedure, and credibility heuristics rather than neutrally **discovering** it.

The bundled thesis claims include performative legal language, **~54.1%** deception-detection accuracy with inverted cue interpretation, memory distortion under suggestive questioning (**~22%** false-memory rates cited in summaries), false confessions in a large share of exonerations, elevated suggestibility after detention, and structural bias against **neurodivergent** defendants whose authentic presentation is misread.

**14 Goals:** **Goal 4** (justice architecture and ViewSwap-style alternatives), **Goal 3** (innocence, detention, confession), **Goal 5** (police interrogation and training), **Goal 6** (reorienting roles toward non-coercive practice).
